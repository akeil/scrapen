13 Travel Products Made for Women That Are Actually Worth Buying

Tried-and-true gear that isn't pink, doesn't sparkle—and makes it easier to be a woman on the road.

By Meredith Carey and Elaheh Nozari

January 19, 2021
    • Save this for later.
    • Save this for later.

All products featured on Condé Nast Traveler are independently selected by our editors. However, when you buy something through our retail links, we may earn an affiliate commission.

Remember the Bic Pen For Her debacle of 2012? Or the "lady-friendly" Doritos idea of 2018? While women don't need pens in “cute” colors or chips with minimal crunch and crumbs, we do want products that address specific needs on the road—like a hiking backpack that properly fits our frame, or a soft, wrinkle-free blouse that easily transitions from the plane to the office (or just the Zoom call). Here are 13 travel products for women that make a frequent traveler's life less stressful and more convenient.

This article was originally published in March 2019. It has been updated with new information.

For the urban explorer

Universal Standard Seine high-rise skinny jeans

Universal Standard is one of our go-tos for basics because of their inclusive sizing and plethora of options. These slightly stretchy skinny jeans, perfect for a day of exploring a new city (or your own backyard) are no different. They come in sizes 00 to 40, five different washes from black to distressed blue, in mid- and high-rise options, and in regular, tall, and petite lengths. Our favorite part of Universal Standard's line is that most of its jeans are eligible for its Fit Liberty program, with lets you exchange your jeans should you go up or down a size within a year of purchase. 

$90 at Universal Standard

Tory Burch color-block crossbody

This Tory Burch bag has a seventies-style color block design that is the epitome of sporty chic. The ripstop nylon, in a mix of half a dozen bright colors, is both durable and lightweight, and a series of pockets—including a front zip pouch, and an interior zip pocket with a card holder—will keep you organized. It's the perfect crossbody for hands-free exploring and the adjustable strap means you can hike it up for urban bike rides and more. 

$168 at Tory Burch

Megababe Thigh Rescue

If you're swapping out jeans for shorts or a dress, female-founded Megababe's thigh chafe stick is a lifesaver on long walks through city streets, no matter the season. The balm-like formula allows your thighs to glide past each other while aloe and pomegranate and grapeseed oils keep your skin hydrated. (We also love the natural, aluminum free deodorant available on Megababe or at Ulta.)

$14 at Megababe
$14 at Ulta

For the adventure traveler

Gregory Packs women's Maven 45

Your height doesn't matter quite as much as you'd think when it comes to finding a hiking backpack that fits; it's your torso length and width that needs to be considered. In theory, you can buy a unisex backpack and hike perfectly well, with some fit help. Gregory, however, makes a pack specifically for a woman's body: It has a contoured hip belt and shoulder straps, and an adjustable frame design. Gregory doesn't offer specifically plus-size packs yet (though a collab with Unlikely Hikers is coming in 2021), but they do offer free belt extenders with any pack if you order directly from the brand, says Unlikely Hikers founder Jenny Bruso. 

$200 at Gregory Packs
$200 at Backcountry

Nike Swoosh pocket sports bra

This sports bra has a mind-blowing number of hidden pockets—including in the chest, band, and where the racerback straps meet—to keep your phone, keys, cards, and more secure on a run. Plus, it's made with sweat-wicking fabric and mesh panels to keep you cool. Choose from black, lavender, and green colorways.

$50 at Nike
$50 at Backcountry

pStyle

When we had Bruso, climber Shelma Jun, and dog musher Blair Braverman on the Women Who Travel podcast, they all agreed on one must-have for anyone who needs to stand to pee: the pStyle. It allows you to relieve yourself standing up—a real plus when you're wearing lots of layers in the winter. “[It's] a piece of women's gear that people ask me about privately all the time,” Braverman told us. Now the secret's out. 

$12 at pStyle

Danner Mountain 600 hiking boot

For light day hikes, the last thing you want is to be weighed down by your boots, plus women's toeboxes are usually a little wider than men's (who'da thought?). This pair weighs just over a pound, putting them in line with sneakers, and features a waterproof suede upper and moisture-wicking interior fabric. Bonus: The soles are made of Vibram rubber for added grip and stability on both dry and wet surfaces. 

$180 at Backcountry

Lululemon Run For it beanie

The Run For it Beanie is a match made in heaven for people with lots of hair: Its best feature is the ponytail opening in the back, which helps ensure the fabric lies flat over your neck and ears during cool runs. The water-repellent cap has tech fleece fabric with reflective details, too, if you prefer to run in the evening.

$38 at Lululemon

For traveling on your period

DivaCup Model 1

If you don't want to be dealing with feminine products on a multi-day camping trip or would rather pack an extra jacket or a pair of shoes than a bulky box of tampons in your suitcase, look to the Diva Cup, a menstrual cup that offers 12 hours of leak-free protection and can be washed out in the shower. Contributor Ashlea Halpern brought it along when packing for a year of travel in a carry-on to make dealing with traveling on her period a breeze. 

$42 at CVS
$21 at Walmart

Thinx Cheeky underwear

If that's too far out of your comfort zone, try Thinx. The company offers anti-microbial, leak-proof underwear that holds the equivalent of two tampons, but looks and feels like a regular pair of underwear. If you're ending your period and want a little extra protection, they come in a thong style as well, which holds the equivalent of half a tampon. To take care of them, just rinse them out in the shower, wash in cold water, and hang to dry. You'll want to pack at least two pairs to alternate.

$30 at Thinx

For the corporate jet-setter

Aday The Monday bundle

If you're looking for a no-fuss outfit that takes you from the plane to the board room—or more likely these days, can get you from a road trip to a Zoom call—Aday has an already bundled uniform with your name on it. The brand's “Monday” uniform comes with the wrinkle-free, breathable Something Borrowed blouse, which is shorter in the front with a Mandarin collar and slit on the side, perfect for a French tuck. It comes in six neutral colors, all with the same loose, slightly oversized fit. Pair it with the bundle's Turn It Up pant that features an elastic waistband for comfort and a tapered leg for immediate style. There are pockets—a must—and the pants come in camel, black, navy, and moss green. 

$298 at Aday (Bundle)
$145 at Aday (Something Borrowed Shirt)
$165 at Aday (Turn It Up Pant)

Dagne Dover 365 Large Landon carryall

This triple-duty bag can work for an overnight business trip, long weekend, or even as a personal item, since it features a back pocket that slips over the top of a suitcase handle. Inside, the soft neoprene bag has Dagne Dover's signature organization system: a 15" padded laptop sleeve, a stretch key leash so they don't get lost, a removable mesh pouch, a nylon shoe/laundry bag, and two side pockets fit for water bottles. Plus, there's an exterior pocket for easy access to your phone or passport. 

$185 at Nordstrom

Dyson Airwrap Complete Styler

Yes, this hair tool comes with a steep price tag—but the reviews show that it actually works. And, its interchangeable tools means you won't have to pack a bulky hair dryer and curling iron and brush sets to get a blow-out look away from home. Using classic Dyson innovation, the six-in-one tool eschews extreme heat, instead using powerful air flow to dry and style your hair at the same time. The full kit includes two different curling barrels (which attract your hair using an air vortex so you don't have to worry about curling evenly or traditional curling iron burns), two smoothing brushes (for both frizz-prone and fine hair types), and a round brush for volume. 

$550 at Best Buy
$550 at Dyson
